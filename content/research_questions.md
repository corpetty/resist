# Resistance Theory: Research Questions & Exploration Guide

## Meta-Questions (Framework-Level)

### Conceptual Foundations

1. **What exactly is "resistance"?**
   - How do we distinguish resistance from crime, terrorism, reform, revolution?
   - Is resistance defined by intent, methods, or outcomes?
   - Can state actions be "resistance"? (e.g., sanctuary cities)

2. **What makes resistance "successful"?**
   - Policy change? Regime change? Cultural shift? Survival?
   - Whose definition of success matters?
   - Can "failed" resistance still be valuable?

3. **Strategic vs. Expressive Action**
   - When is resistance instrumental (aimed at winning)?
   - When is resistance expressive (witness, identity, culture)?
   - Can the same action be both?
   - How do participants understand their own actions?

4. **Agency and Structure**
   - How much do individual choices matter vs. structural conditions?
   - Can tactics create conditions for success, or only exploit existing ones?
   - What's the role of contingency, luck, timing?

### Comparative Questions

5. **What can we actually learn from historical comparisons?**
   - How context-dependent are tactics?
   - What transfers across time/geography?
   - How do we avoid false analogies?

6. **How do resisters learn from each other?**
   - Tactical diffusion across movements
   - Regime learning and counter-tactics
   - Role of advisors, training, literature

## Collective Action Mechanics

### Coordination Problems

7. **What enables coordination at scale?**
   - Communication technology
   - Pre-existing social networks
   - Shared identity/ideology
   - Organizational forms
   - Repeated interaction
   - Which matter most? How do they interact?

8. **How do movements overcome free-riding?**
   - Selective incentives
   - Social sanctions
   - Ideological commitment
   - Making participation visible
   - Small group dynamics
   - What works in different contexts?

9. **Leadership vs. Leaderlessness**
   - Advantages/disadvantages of each
   - Horizontal vs. vertical organization
   - How to maintain discipline without hierarchy?
   - How to maintain flexibility with hierarchy?
   - Hybrid models?

10. **Threshold/Tipping Point Dynamics**
    - What determines critical mass threshold?
    - How do preference cascades work?
    - Role of early adopters vs. mass followers
    - How to trigger tipping points intentionally?

### Sustainability

11. **What maintains participation over time?**
    - Early vs. late adopter motivations
    - Role of victories (small and large)
    - Managing burnout
    - Generational succession
    - Resource flows

12. **How do movements handle success?**
    - Co-optation risks
    - Institutionalization
    - Radicalization vs. moderation
    - Declaring victory vs. continuing struggle

13. **How do movements handle failure?**
    - Regrouping after repression
    - Learning from defeats
    - When to persist vs. pivot
    - Maintaining morale

## Cross-Cutting Themes to Explore

- **Power asymmetry**: How do the powerless challenge the powerful?
- **Timing and contingency**: Role of moments, opportunities, luck
- **Scale**: From individual to mass, local to global
- **Time horizons**: Immediate vs. long-term
- **Visibility vs. security**: Fundamental tension
- **Purity vs. pragmatism**: Ideological commitment vs. winning
- **Expression vs. strategy**: Witness vs. victory
- **Creation vs. destruction**: Building alternatives vs. tearing down
- **Hope vs. realism**: Optimism and its limits

## Methodological Approaches

- **Historical case studies**: Deep dives into specific movements
- **Comparative analysis**: Across time, geography, issue
- **Theoretical frameworks**: Testing and building theory
- **Participant perspectives**: Interviews, memoirs, oral history
- **Quantitative analysis**: When possible and appropriate
- **Process tracing**: Following causal mechanisms
- **Ethnography**: Embedded research
- **Action research**: Research as part of practice
